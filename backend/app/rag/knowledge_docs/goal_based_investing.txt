TITLE: Goal-Based Investing Guide — Indian Context

SOURCE: Financial planning frameworks adapted for Indian investors
APPLICABILITY: Individual investors planning for specific life goals

================================================================================
WHAT IS GOAL-BASED INVESTING?
================================================================================

Goal-based investing means:
- Every rupee invested is tied to a specific life goal
- Asset allocation matches goal's time horizon and risk tolerance
- Progress is measurable (current corpus vs required corpus)
- Prevents emotional selling during market downturns (goal anchor)

Benefits over ad-hoc investing:
- Clarity on how much to invest each month
- Automatic course correction when off-track
- Avoids lifestyle inflation eating into goal savings
- Better discipline: money earmarked for education stays for education

COMMON FINANCIAL GOALS — INDIAN FAMILIES
================================================================================

1. EMERGENCY FUND
   Priority: Highest (Foundation of all financial planning)
   Target: 6 months of living expenses in liquid/accessible form
   Timeline: Immediate (build within 6-12 months)
   Where to keep: Liquid mutual fund, savings account with high interest
   Amount example: Monthly expenses ₹60,000 → Emergency fund ₹3.6 lakh
   Note: Not an investment — a safety net. Never invest emergency fund in equity.

2. CHILD'S EDUCATION
   Priority: High (after emergency fund)
   Timeline: 5-18 years depending on child's age
   Target amount: Very inflation-sensitive
   - Engineering/Medicine (tier 1 private): ₹20-40 lakh today; 15 years → ₹50-100 lakh
   - IIM MBA: ₹25-35 lakh today; 18 years → ₹80-120 lakh
   - Abroad education (USA, UK): ₹60-120 lakh today; 15 years → ₹1.5-3 Crore
   Investment approach:
   - 10+ years: 70-80% equity (flexi cap + mid cap), 20-30% debt
   - 5-10 years: 50-60% equity, 40-50% debt (balanced hybrid)
   - < 5 years: 30% equity max, 70% in short duration/FD
   - Never use ULIP or endowment for child education (low returns, inflexible)
   SIP calculation:
   - ₹50 lakh corpus in 15 years at 12% return: ₹10,000/month SIP
   - ₹1 crore in 15 years at 12%: ₹20,000/month SIP

3. HOME PURCHASE
   Priority: Medium (personal vs investment)
   Timeline: 3-10 years for down payment; then EMI goal
   Down payment: Typically 20% of property value + stamp duty + registration
   - ₹1 Crore flat → ₹20-25 lakh down payment needed
   - 5 years accumulation at 10%: SIP of ₹32,000/month needed
   Investment approach:
   - > 5 years: 50% equity + 50% debt (balanced approach)
   - 3-5 years: Balanced hybrid or balanced advantage fund
   - < 3 years: Short duration debt fund or FD
   Home loan consideration:
   - EMI should not exceed 30-35% of monthly take-home pay
   - Total debt (home + car + personal) < 40-45% of take-home
   - Tax benefit on home loan: 80C (₹1.5L principal, old regime) + 24B (₹2L interest, old regime)
   - Prepay home loan aggressively in first 5-7 years (maximum interest period)

4. RETIREMENT CORPUS
   Priority: High (cannot be deferred)
   Timeline: 15-35 years
   Target: 25-35x annual expenses (depends on lifestyle and returns assumption)
   See dedicated retirement planning guide for detailed calculation.
   Investment approach: Maximum equity early; gradually shift to balance in late career.

5. WEDDING FUND
   Priority: Medium
   Indian wedding costs: ₹10-50 lakh (varies widely)
   Timeline: 5-15 years
   Investment approach:
   - > 7 years: 60% equity, 40% debt
   - 3-7 years: Balanced advantage fund
   - < 3 years: Short duration debt, RD, FD

6. VACATION / TRAVEL
   Priority: Lower (lifestyle goal)
   Timeline: 1-3 years
   Investment approach: Short duration debt fund, recurring deposit, high-yield savings
   Annual international vacation: ₹2-5 lakh → SIP ₹15,000-40,000/month for 1 year

7. VEHICLE PURCHASE
   Priority: Low-medium (depreciating asset — minimise financing)
   Better approach: Save first, buy with minimum loan
   Investment: Short duration fund for 1-2 years; avoid personal loans at 12-18%

GOAL FEASIBILITY SCORING
================================================================================

How to assess if you are on track for a goal:

Feasibility Score 90-100 (On Track):
- Projected corpus ≥ 90% of target
- Current SIP + existing corpus on trajectory to meet goal
- No action needed; maintain current plan
- Recommendation: Continue current SIP; review annually

Feasibility Score 70-89 (Slightly Off Track):
- Small gap between projected and target corpus
- Moderate increase in SIP can close the gap
- Action: Increase monthly SIP by calculated gap amount
- Review in 6 months after SIP increase

Feasibility Score 40-69 (Needs Action):
- Significant shortfall; current plan insufficient
- Options: (1) Increase SIP substantially, (2) Extend timeline, (3) Reduce target
- Immediate action required
- Consider step-up SIP + lumpsum if available

Feasibility Score 0-39 (At Risk):
- Goal unlikely to be met with current trajectory
- Major restructuring needed: increase SIP 2-3x, or significantly extend timeline
- May need to reassess goal priority or combine multiple strategies
- Human advisor review recommended

ASSET ALLOCATION BY GOAL TIMELINE
================================================================================

TIMELINE > 10 YEARS:
Asset allocation: 75-80% equity, 15-20% debt, 5% gold
Reasoning: Long time horizon absorbs market volatility; equity maximises real returns
Suitable instruments: Flexi cap, large+mid cap blend, ELSS, Nifty index fund
Debt component: PPF, NPS, short-medium duration debt fund
Rebalancing: Annual rebalancing to target allocation

TIMELINE 5-10 YEARS:
Asset allocation: 60-70% equity, 25-30% debt, 5-10% gold
Reasoning: Still equity-heavy for growth; debt provides stability as goal approaches
Suitable: Balanced advantage fund + large cap + short duration debt
Rebalancing: Semi-annual or when deviation > 5%

TIMELINE 3-5 YEARS:
Asset allocation: 40-50% equity, 45-50% debt, 5% gold
Reasoning: Approaching goal; reduce equity risk; preserve capital
Suitable: Conservative hybrid fund, arbitrage fund, short duration, FD for stability
Rebalancing: Quarterly; systematically shift to lower risk as goal approaches

TIMELINE 1-3 YEARS:
Asset allocation: 20-30% equity (max), 70-80% debt/liquid
Reasoning: Capital preservation; avoid equity market risk close to goal date
Suitable: Short duration debt fund, FD, liquid fund for 12-18 months
Note: If using equity, ensure it is LTCG qualifying (> 12 months holding)

TIMELINE < 1 YEAR:
Asset allocation: 0-10% equity, 90-100% liquid/short-term debt
Instruments: Liquid fund, ultra-short fund, FD, savings account
Priority: Capital preservation; returns secondary

SIP AMOUNTS FOR COMMON GOALS
================================================================================

(Assuming 12% return for equity, 7% for debt, 6% inflation, 60% equity in portfolio)

CHILD EDUCATION — ₹25 lakh in today's money:
- Timeline 10 years: SIP ≈ ₹12,000/month
- Timeline 15 years: SIP ≈ ₹6,500/month
- Timeline 18 years: SIP ≈ ₹4,500/month (inflation-adjusted target ≈ ₹71 lakh)

HOME DOWN PAYMENT — ₹20 lakh (in today's money):
- Timeline 3 years: SIP ≈ ₹47,000/month (more lumpsum needed)
- Timeline 5 years: SIP ≈ ₹25,000/month
- Timeline 7 years: SIP ≈ ₹16,000/month

RETIREMENT — ₹1 lakh/month in today's money, retire at 60:
- Starting at age 25: SIP ≈ ₹18,000/month
- Starting at age 30: SIP ≈ ₹30,000/month
- Starting at age 35: SIP ≈ ₹52,000/month
- Starting at age 40: SIP ≈ ₹95,000/month
(Dramatic difference — illustrates cost of delay)

WEDDING — ₹20 lakh in today's money:
- Timeline 10 years: SIP ≈ ₹9,000/month
- Timeline 7 years: SIP ≈ ₹15,000/month
- Timeline 5 years: SIP ≈ ₹25,000/month

PRIORITISING GOALS WHEN MONEY IS LIMITED
================================================================================

Priority order for limited resources:
1. Emergency fund (6 months expenses) — non-negotiable
2. Term insurance premium — protect income
3. Health insurance premium — protect against medical costs
4. EPF contribution — mandatory if salaried; do not reduce
5. Retirement SIP — cannot be deferred; compounding most powerful early
6. Child education SIP — long timeline; start as soon as child is born
7. Home down payment — plan well in advance; avoid down payment loan
8. Short-term goals — vacation, vehicle, wedding; fund after above

When under financial stress:
- Do NOT stop retirement SIP (pause or reduce, but don't stop)
- Do NOT withdraw EPF (huge long-term cost)
- Stop short-term discretionary goal SIPs first
- Maintain emergency fund at all costs

================================================================================
GOAL REVIEW AND COURSE CORRECTION
================================================================================

Annual review checklist:
□ Has income changed? → Update SIP proportionally
□ Has family situation changed (marriage, child, parent dependency)? → New goals
□ Has asset allocation drifted from target? → Rebalance
□ Are all goals on track (feasibility score)? → Increase SIP if needed
□ Is emergency fund still adequate? → Replenish if used
□ Has insurance coverage kept pace with liabilities? → Top up term/health

Life events requiring immediate review:
- Marriage: Combine finances; add partner goals; review insurance
- Birth of child: Add education goal; increase life insurance
- Job loss: Stop non-essential SIPs; protect emergency fund
- Salary hike: Increase SIPs proportionally (lifestyle inflation trap)
- Inheritance/bonus: Lumpsum into underperforming goals
- Death of earning member: Life insurance claim → deploy proceeds toward goals
