TITLE: SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013 — Key Provisions for AI Wealth Management

SOURCE: Securities and Exchange Board of India
APPLICABILITY: All SEBI-registered Investment Advisers (IAs) and AI-assisted advisory systems

================================================================================
REGISTRATION AND ELIGIBILITY
================================================================================

Who must register as Investment Adviser:
- Any person providing investment advice for consideration must register with SEBI
- Applies to individuals, partnership firms, body corporates
- Minimum net worth: ₹50 lakh for individuals, ₹150 lakh for non-individuals (as of 2020 amendment)
- Minimum qualification: Professional certification (CFP, CFA, or equivalent) + NISM Series X-A and X-B

Exemptions from registration:
- Insurance agents advising on insurance products only
- Pension advisers advising on NPS/pension only
- Chartered Accountants, Advocates, Company Secretaries providing incidental advice
- Mutual fund distributors (advising only on MF products within their scope)

================================================================================
CONDUCT OBLIGATIONS (Clause 17-24)
================================================================================

Clause 17 — General obligations:
- Act in the best interest of the client at all times
- Disclose all conflicts of interest to the client
- Maintain independence; not accept commissions, referral fees from product manufacturers
- Separate advisory and distribution business

Clause 18 — Risk profiling:
- Mandatory risk profiling of every client before providing advice
- Risk profile must be reviewed at least annually or on material change in client circumstances
- Risk categories: conservative, moderately conservative, moderate, moderately aggressive, aggressive
- Documentation of risk assessment must be retained for 5 years

Clause 19 — Suitability:
- All investment advice must be suitable for the client based on their risk profile
- Adviser must document the rationale for every recommendation
- If AI generates advice, the rationale must be explainable and documented
- Unsuitable advice even if accepted by client is a regulatory violation

Clause 20 — Fiduciary duty:
- Investment Adviser acts as fiduciary — client interest above all
- Cannot place own interests or third-party interests above client's
- Any fee from product manufacturers must be fully disclosed and refunded to client

Clause 21 — Disclosure to clients:
- Disclose: fee structure, conflicts of interest, regulatory status, complaint process
- Provide: Investment Policy Statement (IPS) or financial plan to client
- AI-generated advice: disclose that AI tools are used in the advisory process

Clause 22 — Fees:
- Can charge only advisory fees — no distribution commission
- Fee structure must be disclosed upfront (flat fee, AUM-based, or hourly)
- Cannot accept any other benefit from product manufacturers

Clause 23 — Record keeping:
- Retain all client communication, advice records, rationale for 5 years (Regulation 19)
- Electronic records acceptable if tamper-proof
- AI audit logs count as electronic records under this clause

Clause 24 — Redressal:
- Maintain client complaint register
- Resolve complaints within 30 days
- SEBI SCORES portal for escalation

================================================================================
AI AND TECHNOLOGY IN ADVISORY (SEBI Circular 2023)
================================================================================

SEBI has acknowledged use of AI/ML in investment advisory with conditions:
- AI output must be reviewed by a qualified SEBI-registered IA before client delivery
- Automated advice systems must include suitability checks
- Client must be informed when AI tools are used
- All AI recommendations must have auditable rationale (Clause 19)
- Confidence thresholds: low-confidence AI output must be flagged for human review
- Disclaimer mandatory: AI output is not guaranteed investment advice

Mandatory disclaimer for AI-generated content:
"This information is generated by an AI assistant for educational and informational
purposes only. It does not constitute investment advice under SEBI (Investment Advisers)
Regulations, 2013. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Please consult
your SEBI-registered investment adviser before making any investment decisions."

================================================================================
PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
================================================================================

Investment Advisers must NOT:
- Guarantee returns or promise specific outcomes
- Use words like "assured", "guaranteed", "risk-free" in any communication
- Recommend products without proper suitability assessment
- Churn client portfolio without documented rationale
- Accept gifts, entertainment above ₹1,000 from product manufacturers
- Share client information without consent (except regulatory requirements)
- Provide tips, rumours, or unsubstantiated market information

================================================================================
RECORD RETENTION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

Minimum 5-year retention for:
- Risk profile assessments
- Investment advice given (written/electronic)
- Rationale for each recommendation (Clause 19)
- Client communications (email, chat, SMS)
- AI audit logs (tool calls, confidence scores, disclaimers)
- Fee receipts and invoices
- Complaint records

================================================================================
SEBI INVESTOR PROTECTION FUND (IPF)
================================================================================

Clients may approach:
- SEBI SCORES: scores.sebi.gov.in (online complaint portal)
- SEBI Toll-free: 1800 266 7575
- Investor Service Centres: across major Indian cities
- SMART ODR: Online Dispute Resolution for securities market

================================================================================
KEY DATES AND AMENDMENTS
================================================================================

- 2013: Original IA Regulations enacted
- 2020: Net worth requirements increased; two-tier fee structure removed
- 2021: Investment Adviser must provide one IPS per client per year
- 2023: SEBI circular on AI/ML tools in financial advisory
- 2024: Budget 2024 changes to LTCG/STCG rates — advisers must communicate promptly
